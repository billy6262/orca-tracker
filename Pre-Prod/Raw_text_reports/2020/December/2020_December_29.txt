Strait of Juan de Fuca/Sooke (T233 and T233A) -

13:20-

14:19 -
Center for Whale Research Encounter #74: "… The two killer whales were travelling west-southwest in the Strait at about four knots. They were quickly identified as some rarely encountered outer-coastal Bigg's, T233 and her presumed first offspring, T233A, approximately three years old. After a few sequences of slow meandering travel, the animals hastened the pace, reaching 7-8 knots and travelling in a precise line. …" [See CWR Encounter #74 https://www.whaleresearch.com/2020-74 for full summary and photos]
