Orca Sightings for May 6, 2009
============================================================

Entry 1:
At

3:15 p.m.
encountered
J Pod
(plus other orcas) travelling east at the coal docks north of
point Roberts
. My naturalist Tammy identified Polaris but the whales were spread out and no other definite i.d.'s
Captain Rich Massey, Vancouver Whale Watch
*
I am still listening to calls on the Orcasound hydrophone at

12:20 a.m.
Sharon Grace, San Juan Island
*
Orca Network received a call from Jim Peters, reporting a whale that sounded like it was possibly the
Humpback
sighted yesterday in Seattle - at
1 pm
in
Quartermaster Harbor
, between Vashon & Maury Islands. The whale was between the Burton Peninsula and Dockton Park, heading north into the Harbor.
*
Orca Network received a call from Bill Gates of the OR Dept. of Fish & Wildlife, reporting
2 adult orcas
(3-4' tall fins) swimming quickly upstream in the
Umpqua River, OR
adjacent to the USCG Station, ~2 miles upstream at 1 pm today. He said there were many sea lions in the river.
*
At about

3:30 PM
today there was a
gray whale
working its way north between Van Dam & Hastie Lake (
NW Whidbey Isl
) about 100 yards off shore. My neighbor alerted me & I was thrilled because this was my first sighting since moving into this house 4 yrs. ago.
Shirley Taft, Oak Harbor, Whidbey Island
*
Nadja Baker of Anacortes called at

12:45 pm
to report a Gray whale ~ 1/2 mile offshore of Sunset/West Beach, NW Whidbey Island, heading south.
*
Jerry Pitsch of Oak Harbor called at
8 am
to report
2 Gray whales
off West Beach, NW Whidbey Island, no real direction of travel at the time. He said they were there all day Sunday,
May 3rd. He said the larger whale is a darker color, and has a white spot midway back on its right side.
